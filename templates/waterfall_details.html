Plan Your Visit

Experience the magic of nature's most beautiful cascade.


What to Carry

  • • Waterproof shoes or trekking sandals
  • • Extra set of clothes (you will get wet!)
  • • Waterproof bag for phone/camera
  • • Towel & dry snacks
  • • Sunscreen and insect repellent
  • • Drinking water (2 liters)
  • • Raincoat (monsoon essential)

⚠️ Water Safety Rules

  • • Never swim during heavy rain - flash floods risk
  • • Check water current before entering pools
  • • Don't venture near waterfall edge - rocks are slippery
  • • Swim only in designated safe pools
  • • Keep children under constant supervision
  • • Avoid jumping from rocks or heights

🌧️ When to Visit

Peak Monsoon (July-Aug):

Full force, thundering cascade - most dramatic but avoid swimming

Post-Monsoon (Sep-Nov):

Perfect balance - good water flow + safe for swimming

Winter (Dec-Feb):

Reduced flow, crystal clear pools - best for photography

💡 Local Experience

  • • Food Stalls: Try fresh corn bhaji & sugarcane juice from local vendors
  • • Best Route: Ask villagers for the "old forest path" - more scenic!
  • • Parking: Local guides charge ₹50-100 for vehicle watch - fair price
  • • Swimming Spots: Lower pool is safer than the main waterfall base

🌿 Be a Responsible Visitor

  • • Don't use soap/shampoo in waterfall pools
  • • Carry back ALL plastic & waste - including wrappers
  • • Don't carve names or damage rocks
  • • Support local vendors - buy snacks from them
  • • Keep music low - let nature's sounds prevail
